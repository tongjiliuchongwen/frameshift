# v0.3.1 Human Trial Protocol

This protocol turns the Method-Grid QD-lite demo into a short real-use trial.
The goal is not to prove research quality. The goal is to observe whether the
workflow helps a researcher find problem entrances they would not normally
explore.

## Trial Goal

Use one rough research idea and answer:

```text
Does the method grid reveal useful, grounded reframing directions that the user
would not have reached through direct brainstorming?
```

## Duration

Plan for 20 to 30 minutes.

## Materials

- `input.md`
- `outputs/03_method_grid.md`
- `outputs/04_method_grid.html`
- `outputs/04_method_qd_archive.md`
- `outputs/06_idea_cards.md`
- `outputs/human_trial_notes_template.md`

## Procedure

1. Read `input.md` and confirm that it represents the rough idea being tested.
2. Open `outputs/04_method_grid.html` or read `outputs/03_method_grid.md`.
3. Pick 3 occupied cells that look promising.
4. Pick 2 empty or underexplored cells that should be explored in a future run.
5. From the occupied cells, pick 2 elite candidates that are worth turning into
   deeper research plans.
6. Record the reasons for each selection in `human_trial_notes_template.md`.
7. Answer the trial questions below.

## Trial Questions

1. Which candidates felt like ideas you would not have produced yourself?
2. Which candidates felt like paraphrases of the original idea?
3. Which cells would you want the agent to explore next?
4. Could you understand where each candidate came from?
5. Did you trust the quality scores? Why or why not?
6. Would you push any candidate into a research plan?
7. What was the largest friction point in the flow?

## Evaluation Notes

Treat quality scores as prompts for inspection, not proof. The trial should
prioritize human selection confidence, trace clarity, and willingness to keep
working with a candidate.

## Stop Conditions

Stop the trial early if:

- the user cannot understand how cells and candidates relate,
- candidates repeatedly feel ungrounded,
- the HTML view does not help selection,
- or the user cannot identify any next action.

Record the stop reason in the notes template.
